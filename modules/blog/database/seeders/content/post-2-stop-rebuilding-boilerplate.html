If you've launched more than one Laravel application, you know the feeling. The idea is clear, the vision is sharp, and yet somehow the first two weeks are always the same: authentication, password resets, email verification, admin panels, billing integration, dark mode, responsive layouts, CI/CD pipelines.

You've built it all before. You'll build it again. And it never gets faster.

This is the problem Saucebase was built to solve.

The problem with boilerplate

Every SaaS has a core set of requirements that aren't your competitive advantage. Nobody chooses your product because you wrote a particularly elegant password reset flow. Nobody stays because your admin panel has custom breadcrumbs. These things need to exist — they need to work well — but they shouldn't cost you weeks of development time.

What Saucebase gives you on day one

  • Auth module — registration, login, social OAuth, 2FA, email verification
  • Billing module — Stripe subscriptions, plan management, trials, and webhooks
  • Settings module — profile, email, password, notification preferences
  • Blog module — categories, cover images, SEO metadata, Filament admin (you're reading it now)

Each module is self-contained and copy-and-own. You get the source code, not a locked dependency. Modify it to fit your needs, extend it with your own logic, or strip it down to the essentials. The code is yours.

The result

Your CI pipeline is configured. Your tests are written. Your admin panel is live. Your frontend is dark-mode aware and SSR-optimised. Your TypeScript types are generated from your PHP.

You didn't have to think about any of that. You can spend those first two weeks building the thing that actually makes your SaaS worth using.

That's the promise of Saucebase: skip the setup, ship the product.
